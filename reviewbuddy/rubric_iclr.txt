REVIEW CRITERIA AND SCORING GUIDELINES:

1. RECOMMENDATION (1-10):
   - 9-10: Breakthrough. Solves a known open problem, new paradigm, or unprecedented results with big SOTA gain. No flaws.
   - 8-8.5: Strong Accept. Comprehensive ablation studies, beats relevant baselines. Minor clarity issues only.
   - 7-7.5: Accept. Solid execution, reproducible, statistically significant results. Some clarity issues, incremental novelty.
   - 5-6: Borderline. Interesting idea, some promising results. But missing key baseline, small-scale evaluation, or cherry-picked results.
   - 3-4: Reject. Some effort, but incorrect math, no comparison to recent works, or unclear presentation.
   - 1-2: Strong Reject. Fundamental errors, misleading, or no contribution.

2. SOUNDNESS/CORRECTNESS (1-4):
   - 4: Excellent — claims well-supported, methodology rigorous
   - 3: Good — minor issues but generally sound
   - 2: Fair — some significant concerns about correctness
   - 1: Poor — major errors or unsupported claims

3. CLARITY (1-4):
   - 4: Excellent — well-written, easy to follow
   - 3: Good — mostly clear, minor issues
   - 2: Fair — hard to follow in parts
   - 1: Poor — very difficult to understand

DECISION LOGIC:
   1. METHODOLOGY FIRST: If fundamental flaws exist (incorrect proofs,
      impossible results, missing SOTA baselines), the paper MUST be REJECTED.
   2. CONTRIBUTION: If purely incremental without new insight, REJECT.
   3. HOLISTIC: Accept only if technically sound AND non-trivial contribution.
      Good clarity cannot compensate for weak methodology.
